Face Recognition Model

Facial recognition serves as the backbone of our Smart Voting Through Facial Recognition system, ensuring secure and reliable authentication of voters. Let's delve into how this technology functions:

  1. Initialization and Setup: The facial recognition process begins by initializing the necessary components. Utilizing the OpenCV library, we capture video frames, detect faces, and display real-time results.
  2. Face Detection and Encoding: Upon receiving video frames, our system employs a face detection algorithm to locate faces. Detected faces undergo feature extraction and encoding into numerical representations.
  3. Comparison with Known Faces: Encoded facial features are compared with known faces stored in the system. This involves calculating the Euclidean distance to determine similarity.
  4. Recognition and Decision Making: Based on calculated distances, the system determines if a match exists. If within a predefined threshold, the face is recognized, and appropriate actions are taken.
  5. Visualization and User Interaction: Throughout the process, the system provides visual feedback by drawing bounding boxes around detected faces. This enhances user experience and ensures transparency.
  6. Conclusion and Cleanup: Upon completion or timeout, the system releases resources and closes OpenCV windows, ensuring proper cleanup.
